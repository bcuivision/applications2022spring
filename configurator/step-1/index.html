VANS CUSTOMS

9 models of product for selection

Strengths

  • The ability to customize sneaker with self-selected art or images is innovative
  • Image manipulation features such as patterning
  • An abundance of user options including highlights, solids, and patterns
  • Live chat functionality
  • Start over functionality

Weakness

  • Too many steps before actually customizing the sneaker, which means that users may lose their patience while customizing
  • Not spontaneous, some functionality like embroidery takes a long time to visualize
  • The left/ right arrows allowing the user to navigate between steps of customization are not efficient and accessible. Placing navigation on the lower-left corner is counterintuitive for users. Instead, the navigation should be given a higher priority.
  • Livechat and add-to-cart icons should take a lower priority in the website because they are secondary when customizing.
  • The organization of information does not demonstrate clarity, because there are too many choices in color and textures.  

Design Your Own Converse

Strengths

  • Contrary to the previous two sneaker configurator, Converse utilizes a responsive 2d mockup with multiple perspectives (in a carousel) instead of a 360 degree 3d mockup. This approach demonstrates clarity at a lower cost in technology.  
  • Hover states and highlights shoe parts along with labels for different shoe parts directly indicates which part of the sneaker the users are customizing, consequently achieving clarity and accessibility.

Weakness

  • Accessibility and clarity can be improved for first-time users. Perhaps a tutorial/ instruction overlay explaining the different functionalities would be helpful for first-time users.
  • The lack of numbering and labeling of different shoe parts on the customizing tab of the mobile version hinders clarity.
  • Lack of visual continuity in the navigation transitions. When entering the customize page, I think having a pop-up/slide-up transition might be helpful.
  • The placement of the customization column on the left should be moved to the left to better user flow. Since we read from left to right, we see the things on the left before the things on the right. Seeing the customization before the live configurator would also provide context for the user, thus increasing clarity.
