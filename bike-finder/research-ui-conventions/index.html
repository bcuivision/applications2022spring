Concluding Summaries

What elements or patterns seem universal?

  • In all four of the websites we observed, the user journey starts from the home page with a navigation bar.
  • Once we go to the secondary page, there’s always a filter feature.
  • The call to action entrances to the next step of the user journey is highlighted to prioritize their place in the visual hierarchy.

Did I see anything that felt different or unusual?

  • The pop-up prompting for email sign and contact broker on the YachtWorld website felt unexpected, which we didn’t see on other shopping sites.

What elements or patterns were specific to the store or product?

  • The comparison feature was specific to Apple AirPods, and we compared them, as they fall into the same category by function.
  • Even though all four shopping sites have somewhat a filter feature that enables the user to narrow down their search, each filter was specific to the store, as they sell different products, so they filter by various categories.

Were there any patterns or techniques that made one store stand out? Could the other stores have applied that technique successfully?

  • The unexpected email pop-up from the YachtWorld website stands out incorrectly. They felt invasive and aggressive, consequently creating a bad customer experience.
  • This straightforward sales strategy felt cheap, so I wouldn’t recommend other sites to apply this forceful antipattern.  

Finally, how did you feel when interacting with these sites? When were you happiest/most comfortable? When were you disrupted/confused/bothered?

  • Overall, my shopping experience on all four sites felt comfortable and intuitive. However, the unexpected email popup on the YachtWorld website bothered me, as it felt invasive.
  • All four sites demonstrate a precise organization of information on the navigation bar, making the interaction smooth and comfortable. Yet, I would note that Apple’s navigation bar, along with the drop-down menu, seems overwhelmed due to the enormous and distinct amount of product info it contains.
